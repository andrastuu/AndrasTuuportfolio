Thesis: Exploring Vision Transformers for Galaxy Classification

My thesis investigates the effectiveness of Vision Transformers (ViTs) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for galaxy morphological classification. Utilizing datasets like Galaxy10SDSS and Galaxy10DECals, the study explores advanced image preprocessing and model optimization techniques to enhance classification accuracy and efficiency in astronomical datasets.

Thesis Summary

This study aims to improve galaxy morphological classification by leveraging Vision Transformers for image-based classification, achieving high accuracy across various preprocessing methods. Key findings include the superior performance of ViTs for certain galaxy categories and the effectiveness of Non-Local Means Denoising for noisy astronomical images.
